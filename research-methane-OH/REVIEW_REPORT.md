## 3. Comparative Evaluation: Which Is Most Authoritative?

### Ranking by Comprehensiveness

1. **Global Methane Budget (Saunois et al. 2020, 2025)** — *Most comprehensive overall*
   - Synthesizes both top-down (inverse) and bottom-up (process) estimates
   - Combines multiple model results, satellite data, and surface networks
   - Explicitly quantifies OH sink term with uncertainty
   - Freely available with supplement data tables
   - **Limitation**: OH is a derived quantity, not the primary focus

2. **CCMI-1 (Zhao et al. 2019; Nicely et al. 2020)** — *Most comprehensive model-based OH database*
   - 18 chemistry-climate models with consistent protocol
   - Full 3D OH fields archived
   - Detailed budget decomposition and driver attribution
   - **Publicly downloadable** from BADC CCMI archive
   - **Limitation**: Model spread is large (±15% in global mean OH)

3. **AerChemMIP/CMIP6 (Stevenson et al. 2020)** — *Most authoritative for historical trends*
   - State-of-the-art Earth System Models
   - Includes sensitivity experiments isolating specific drivers
   - Covers 1850–2014 with transient simulations
   - Available through CMIP6 ESGF nodes
   - **Limitation**: Only 3 core models; fewer than ACCMIP/CCMI

4. **AGAGE/NOAA MCF-derived OH** — *Gold standard for observational constraint*
   - Decades of high-quality measurements
   - Provides independent check on models
   - **Limitation**: MCF abundances declining, reducing future utility; constrains only global/hemispheric mean

5. **ACCMIP (Naik et al. 2013; Voulgarakis et al. 2013)** — *Historical benchmark*
   - 16 models, widely cited
   - **Limitation**: Superseded by CCMI and AerChemMIP; uses older emissions

### 4.2 Critical Revisions to Existing Knowledge

1. **The "15% OH bias" in models** (Prather2024Science): Most CCMI/AerChemMIP models overestimate OH by ~15% compared to MCF-derived values. The UV H₂O absorption mechanism, combined with tropospheric halogen chemistry, provides a plausible resolution. **This is the single most impactful finding for updating OH databases.**

2. **The Global Methane Budget imbalance** (He2026Science; Saunois2025ESSD): The latest TROPOMI inversions (2019–2024) suggest the CH₄ growth is primarily an approach to steady state, not a sudden emission surge — a major revision to earlier interpretations.

3. **Space-based OH observing strategy** (Duncan2024ACP): Satellite proxies can now constrain ~75% of global OH production and ~50% of sinks. This represents a paradigm shift from the MCF-era of global-mean-only constraints toward spatially resolved OH fields.

## 6. Conclusions & Recommendations

1. **No single "definitive" OH database exists.** The CCMI-1 archive (Zhao et al. 2019) is the most comprehensive model-based 3D OH database publicly available. The Global Methane Budget (Saunois et al. 2020, 2025) is the most authoritative for the CH₄ sink quantification.

2. **Urgent model updates needed.** The UV H₂O absorption mechanism (Prather2024Science) and tropospheric halogen chemistry should be incorporated into all models before the next intercomparison (CCMI-2022).

3. **The MCF era is ending.** As CH₃CCl₃ concentrations decline, satellite-based proxy methods (Anderson, Souri, Duncan, Zhao) represent the future of OH monitoring.

4. **The 43 papers collectively provide**: updated emission inventory impacts, revised hemispheric OH gradients, new photochemical mechanisms, satellite proxy validation, and the latest (2026) CH₄ trend attribution — all of which supplement and in some cases **revise** existing databases.
